Yakisoba

Description

Yakisoba is a classic Japanese stir-fried noodle dish that's seasoned with a sweet and savory sauce similar to Worcestershire sauce. Use any proteins you like: pork, chicken, shrimp, or calamari. For vegetarians, just swap it with tofu or shiitake mushrooms.

Ingredients

  • ½ onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 3 shiitake mushrooms
  • 2 green onions
  • 4 cabbage leaves
  • 340g sliced pork belly
  • 2Tbsp natural-flavored oil
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 servings yakisoba noodles
  • 4-6Tbsp Yakisoba Sauce

For the Yakisoba Sauce (Makes ½ cup, 120 ml)

  • 4Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 4tsp oyster sauce
  • 4tsp ketchup
  • 2tsp soy sauce
  • 2-4tsp sugar

For the Toppings(Optional)

  • aonori
  • pickled red ginger

Steps

  1. Gather all the ingredients.

To Make the Yakisoba Sauce

  1. Whisk together all the ingredients for the Yakisoba Sauce. It's important to taste the sauce and see if you need to add more sugar. For example, some ketchup is sweeter than others while some Worcestershire sauce is less sour than others. Set it aside.

To Make the Yakisoba

  1. Slice the onion, cut the carrot into julienne strips, and slice the shiitake mushrooms.
  2. Chop the green onions into 2-inch (5 cm) pieces, cut the cabbage into bite-sized pieces, and cut the meat into 1-inch (2.5 cm) pieces.
  3. In a skillet or wok, heat the oil on medium-high heat. Cook the meat until it's no longer pink.
  4. Add the onions and carrots and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add the cabbage and cook until it's almost tender.
  6. Lastly, add the green onions and shiitake mushrooms and cook for 1 minute. Season with freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  7. Transfer the yakisoba noodles to a sieve and quickly run hot water over the noodles. Loosen the noodles with your hands. Add the noodles to the skillet or wok and lower the heat to medium. It's best to use tongs to combine the noodles and ingredients. Keep an eye on the noodles as they may stick to the skillet/wok.
  8. Add the Yakisoba Sauce and mix it all together. Add more sauce if needed.

To Serve

  1. Transfer the yakisoba to individual plates. Garnish with the dried green seaweed and pickled red ginger. Serve immediately.

To Store

  1. You can store any leftovers in an airtight container and store in the refrigerator for up to 3 days and in the freezer for a month. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and reheat in the microwave to warm.
